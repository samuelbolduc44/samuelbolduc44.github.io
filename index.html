About Me

I am an applied mathematician specialising in nonlinear dynamics, scientific computing, and conceptual climate modelling. My research focuses on infinite-dimensional dynamical systems, particularly delay differential equations (DDEs), to understand the fundamental mechanisms driving climate variability. By combining rigorous mathematical proofs with advanced numerical methods, I isolate and analyse the elementary physical processes that govern complex climate interactions. My core expertise lies in analytical and numerical bifurcation theory, resonance phenomena, and semiflow dynamics.

Beyond research, I have extensive experience tutoring undergraduate mathematics and regularly present my findings at academic conferences.

My technical toolkit includes Julia, MATLAB, and Java, alongside numerical continuation software like DDE-BifTool and custom-built simulators for ordinary, delay, and state-dependent delay differential equations.

PROJECTS

Julia code to compute rotation numbers of the periodically forced delay differential equation

This project computes the rotation numbers of the pfS&S DDE via stroboscopic-section in a two parameters space. Loosely, the rotation number $\rho$ is the ratio between the frequencies of the two interacting oscillations at play: the external forcing (period $T_{\omega} = 2\pi/\gamma$) and the system's own delay-induced oscillation. On an attracting invariant torus, $\rho$ characterizes the type of motion on the torus — rational $\rho = p/q$ corresponds to mode locking (a periodic orbit that closes after $q$ forcing periods), irrational $\rho$ corresponds to quasiperiodic motion that never exactly closes.

Compute local max of pfDDE

This repository contains a Julia script for generating bifurcation diagrams of a scalar (potentially non-autonomous) delay differential equation. The script solves the delay differential equation for a sequence of delay values and records the local maxima of the long-time solution to construct a classical bifurcation diagram.

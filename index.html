About Me

I am an applied mathematician specialising in nonlinear dynamics, scientific computing, and conceptual climate modelling. My research focuses on infinite-dimensional dynamical systems, particularly delay differential equations (DDEs), to understand the fundamental mechanisms driving climate variability. By combining rigorous mathematical proofs with advanced numerical methods, I isolate and analyse the elementary physical processes that govern complex climate interactions. My core expertise lies in analytical and numerical bifurcation theory, resonance phenomena, and semiflow dynamics.

Beyond research, I have extensive experience tutoring undergraduate mathematics and regularly present my findings at academic conferences.

My technical toolkit includes Julia, MATLAB, and Java, alongside numerical continuation software like DDE-BifTool and custom-built simulators for ordinary, delay, and state-dependent delay differential equations.
